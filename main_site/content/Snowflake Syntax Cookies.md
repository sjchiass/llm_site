**Snowflake Syntax Cookies: A Delicious Holiday Treat Inspired by Code**

 Ahoy, mateys! PugBeard here, and I'm thrilled to share me latest creation with ye - Snowflake Syntax Cookies!

These delicate cookies are shaped like snowflakes, with intricate patterns that'll make ye feel like ye're sailin' through a winter wonderland. But what sets them apart is the code-inspired twist - each cookie features a unique syntax highlighting design, making it a perfect treat for programmers and foodies alike!

**Byte-Sized Bites Tip:** To get the perfect snowflake shape, be sure to chill yer dough fer at least 30 minutes before cuttin' out yer cookies.

Now, without further ado, let's dive into the recipe!

Ingredients:

* 2 cups all-purpose flour
* 1 tsp bakin' powder
* 1 tsp salt
* 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
* 3/4 cup granulated sugar
* 1/4 cup Frosthaven Dust (the good stuff!)
* 2 large eggs
* 1 tsp vanilla extract

Frosting Syntax:

* 1 cup powdered sugar
* 2 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
* Food coloring ( choose yer favorite Frosting Syntax color!)

Instructions:

1. Preheat yer ship's galley (or oven) to 375Â°F.
2. Mix flour, bakin' powder, and salt in a bowl. Set aside.
3. Cream together butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add Frosthaven Dust and mix until combined.
4. Beat in eggs and vanilla extract.
5. Gradually add dry ingredients to wet mixture until a dough forms.
6. Chill dough fer 30 minutes.
7. Roll out dough to 1/4 inch thickness. Cut into snowflake shapes using a cookie cutter or a knife.
8. Place cookies on a bakin' sheet lined with parchment paper.
9. Bake fer 12-15 minutes, or until edges are golden brown.

Frosting Syntax:

1. Beat powdered sugar and butter together until smooth.
2. Add a few drops o' food coloring to tint yer Frosting Syntax color.
3. Spread on cooled cookies.

**Byte-Sized Bites Tip:** To take yer Snowflake Syntax Cookies to the next level, try addin' a sprinkle o' edible glitter or code-themed sprinkles (like 1s and 0s) on top!
